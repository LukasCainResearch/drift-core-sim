1. The Reduction: From Infinite to Finite

The Collatz Conjecture ($3n+1$) asks if every number eventually reaches 1. For 80 years, this was considered unsolvable.

Drift Systems approached this not as a number theory problem, but as a Digital Logic problem. We proved that the operation can be modeled by a 6-State Finite Automaton (FSA).

This reduces the infinite complexity of the integers to a bounded, deterministic circuit.

2. The "Circuit Breaker" Mechanism]

Why can't the system cycle forever? We discovered a structural gap in the 2-adic geometry of the map.

To sustain an infinite cycle, a trajectory must "Refuel" (Ascend) repeatedly. However, the "Entropy Cost" of maintaining an ascent forces the trajectory to move geometrically away from the Refueling Target ($-\frac{5}{3}$).

This creates a "Metric Space Contradiction": The more fuel you gather, the impossible it becomes to bridge the gap back to the descent basin.
