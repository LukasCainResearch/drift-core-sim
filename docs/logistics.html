Logistics

Time-bounded liveness and synchronization for distributed operations: reject replay/tamper, classify delay vs compromise, and recover using quorum policy across fleets, hubs, and suppliers.

VIEW DEMOS CONTACT

1. Overview

What it solves:

  • Replay/stale updates across handoffs (scan events, EDI, telemetry)
  • Tamper detection for on-path message manipulation (status, routing, custody)
  • Delay classification: benign latency vs adversarial disruption
  • Quorum continuity for degraded modes and controlled recovery

How to read the demos: they demonstrate invariants (freshness, timing, quorum) using tutorial math, not proprietary construction or production keys.

DECISION INVARIANTS

Fresh challenge + strict deadline window + expected/received comparison → PASS/FAIL/TIMEOUT. Policy maps these outcomes to isolate, degrade, reroute, or recover.

2. Instantiations

Chain of custody
Integrity
Custody handoff verification

Enforce freshness and timing at transfer points (dock, hub, carrier) to prevent replayed or altered custody events.

Fleet operations
Mesh
Quorum continuity for dispatch

Maintain safe degraded operation under partition (connectivity loss, spoofed nodes) and recover by policy when peers agree.

Warehouse systems
Sync
Deadline-bounded synchronization

Distinguish late-but-valid events from suspect traffic; route TIMEOUT outcomes into resync workflows instead of silent acceptance.

Supplier network
Resilience
Policy-based recovery

Use quorum thresholds and recovery rules to isolate compromised edges while keeping the network operational.
